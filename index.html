Hello.

My name is Ketib Oldiais ('kuhtib oldy-eyes'). I studied philosophy at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign, focusing primarily on mathematical logic and computation. I later taught philosophy and mathematics before entering law school. I enjoy writing and reading programs that automate the mundane, alongside articles on data structures and algorithms, mathematics, and jurisprudence. Some potpourri:

  • Areas of current interest: Programming languages, data structures and algorithms, law and computation.
  • Working languages: C, ML, JavaScript, C++, Java, C#, Python, Swift.
  • Familiar languages: Haskell, Racket, Scheme, Rust, Verilog, Go, Matlab.
  • Frameworks/Libraries: Django, React, D3.js, Three.js, KaTeX
  • Daily Tools: Vim, Emacs, LaTeX

  • Ketib Oldiais, Anderson-Burdick, Democracy, and Tradition in the Republic of Palau, Wis. L. Rev. (2021)

    A comment I wrote for the Wisconsin Law Review. The article presents an analytical framework for resolving disputes between the Republic of Palau's traditional and democratic governments. Modeled after the Anderson-Burdick Doctrine from American election law, the framework calls for a balancing test informed by empirical observation.
