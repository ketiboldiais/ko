Hello.

My name is Ketib Oldiais ('kuhtib oldy-eyes'). I studied philosophy at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign — focusing primarily on mathematical logic and computation — and later taught philosophy and mathematics as an adjuct instructor. After my brief stint standing on the other end of a classroom, I entered law school and served as an articles editor for the Wisconsin Law Review. I enjoy writing and reading programs that automate the mundane, alongside articles on data structures and algorithms, mathematics, and jurisprudence. Some potpourri:

  • Ketib Oldiais, Anderson-Burdick, Democracy, and Tradition in the Republic of Palau, 6 Wis. L. Rev. 1581 (2021)

    A comment I wrote for the Wisconsin Law Review, published in Volume Six, December 2021. The article presents an analytical framework for resolving disputes between the Republic of Palau's traditional and democratic governments. Modeled after the Anderson-Burdick Doctrine from American election law, the framework calls for a balancing test informed by empirical observation.
