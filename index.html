Hello.

My name is Ketib Oldiais ('kuhtib oldy-eyes'). I studied philosophy at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign, focusing primarily on mathematical logic and computation. I taught philosophy and mathematics for a brief period before entering law school. These days, I spend most of my time writing programs for automating the mundane, alongside articles on data structures, algorithms, and mathematics. Areas of current interest: Programming languages, algorithms, law and computation.

Publications

  • Ketib Oldiais, Anderson-Burdick, Democracy, and Tradition in the Republic of Palau, Wis. L. Rev. (2021)

    A comment I wrote for the Wisconsin Law Review. The article presents an analytical framework for resolving disputes between the Republic of Palau's traditional and democratic governments. Modeled after the Anderson-Burdick Doctrine from American election law, the framework calls for a balancing test informed by empirical observation.
